I am a PhD student in the INRIA Saclay team OPIS, working under the supervision of Emilie Chouzenoux and Víctor Elvira. Currently, I try to minimize statistical divergences such as the Rényi divergences using ideas from information geometry and optimization methods based on Bregman divergences. We aim at leveraging these results to design efficient algorithms and better understand existing ones in the fields of adaptive importance sampling, global optimization, and variational inference.

Short bio

In 2020, I graduated from Ensta Paris and from the Université Paris-Saclay Optimization master programme. During my master's thesis, I have worked with Alain Rapaport in the MISTEA team in Montpellier on questions related to the study and control of the periodic chemostat model. This model represents micro-organisms competing for a single limiting ressource that is fed in a periodic manner. I have then continued working on related biological models with Jérôme Harmand for a short-term research engineer position at the LBE in Narbonne. I also interned at the UTFSM in Valparaíso, where I studied under the direction of Cristofer Hermosilla and Pedro Gajardo the continuity properties of the set of sustainable thresholds, a set-valued operator linked to viability kernels.
